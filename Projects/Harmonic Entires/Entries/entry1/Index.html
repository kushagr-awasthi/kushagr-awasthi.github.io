Divorce and Democracy: A History of Personal Law in India

by Saumya Saxena

Divorce and Democracy is a recently released book by Saumya Saxena, a post-doctoral fellow at Jesus College, University of Cambridge. It illustrates the manner in which Indian personal law remains non-universal: with separate statutes in place, segregated by religion. It explores this dynamic interplay between law, religion, family, minority rights and gender in Indian politics.
