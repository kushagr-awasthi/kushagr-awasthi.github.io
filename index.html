About the Project

Every time I fly, crossing oceans and continents, to study at Parsons in New York, I know, with some dejection, that I am entering a world where time is a scarce resource and cultural and intellectual ruminations are to become a much procrastinated luxury.

I really miss reading, it is a love and passion that has suffered the most upon the altar of a "world-class-education." But sacrifices are necessary for success, and many would say I am lucky to be able to pursue even one passion in the course of a career.

But it is a sacrifice, nonetheless, grudgingly made: books have been my friend. A steady companion through dreaded years of high school: a source of solace in solitude.

One of my favourite quotes was used to describe the Indian Artist Nandalal Bose, and I paraphrase, "Aesthetic is an ethos: it is not simply life-enhancing, it is world-shaping."

I have always endeavoured to shape the world using design, my work is lead by an irreverent hubris that allows me to imagine impractically and improbably to create projects that seem to large for an undergrad student to even have the gall to concieve of.

But in not knowing the work of author-artists, I miss out on the examples of world building that has been undertaken for centuries, thus my harmonic entries project aims to list the books and works I wish to read; those which I might one day have the pleasure and temporal space to truly savour.
